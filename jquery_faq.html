Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone is the first national park in the United States, established in 1872.
  • It spans across three states: Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho.
  • The park is famous for its geothermal features, including the iconic Old Faithful geyser.
  • Yellowstone is home to diverse wildlife, including grizzly bears, wolves, and herds of bison and elk.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is one of the seven natural wonders of the world.
  • It is located in Arizona and is known for its breathtaking and expansive views.
  • The canyon is approximately 277 miles (446 km) long, up to 18 miles (29 km) wide, and over a mile (6,093 feet or 1,857 meters) deep.
  • Visitors can explore the canyon by hiking its trails, riding a mule, or taking a helicopter tour.

Yosemite National Park

  • Yosemite is located in California's Sierra Nevada mountains and covers an area of about 748,436 acres.
  • The park is known for its towering granite cliffs, including the iconic El Capitan and Half Dome.
  • Yosemite Valley, carved by glaciers, is a popular destination within the park, offering stunning waterfalls such as Yosemite Falls and Bridalveil Fall.
  • The park is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, offering opportunities for hiking, rock climbing, camping, and wildlife viewing.
